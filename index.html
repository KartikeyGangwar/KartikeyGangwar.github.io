Focus

I formalized Solution Manifold Incompatibility (SMI) — a structural limitation of linear multi-fidelity methods for nonlinear PDEs. The work demonstrates that linear fusion of solutions from distinct nonlinear operators is mathematically inadmissible, regardless of optimization quality or model capacity.

My approach is implementation-driven: building computational experiments, isolating systematic failure modes, then formalizing observations through operator-theoretic arguments.

Solution Manifold Incompatibility in Multi-Fidelity PINNs
Kartikey Singh
DOI: 10.5281/zenodo.17794638 | Code: GitHub

Introduces the SMI framework with analytical proofs of operator non-closure under linear fusion. Presents numerical experiments across Allen–Cahn, Burgers, and Navier–Stokes equations. Includes open-source implementation and diagnostic metrics.
